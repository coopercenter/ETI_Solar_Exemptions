Virginia Solar Energy Equipment and Facilities Property Tax Exemptions by Locality

Tax exemptions can be a powerful tool to incentivize and promote solar energy investments. In accordance with state code 58.1-3661, Virginia localities may exempt wholly or partially from taxation certified solar energy equipment. This tool provides an interactive summary of the policies for localities that reported they offer a full or partial solar energy equipment exemption in Weldon Cooper Center’s 2019 Virginia Local Tax Rates Survey.

Fairfax County Full The amount of the exemption provided by this Section shall be determined by applying the local tax rate to the value of the certified solar energy equipment, facilities or devices and subtracting one hundred (100) per cent of such amount from the total real estate property tax due on the real property to which such equipment, facilities or devices are attached. Exemption value will be converted to a tax credit and deducted from the property's tax bill every year for 5 years. Municipal Code fairfaxcounty.gov

Hanover County Partial The exemption provided by this section shall be determined by applying the local tax rate to the value of such equipment, facilities or devices and subtracting one-half of such amount either: (1)From the total real property tax due on the real property to which such equipment, facilities, or devices are attached, or (2)If such equipment, facilities, or devices are taxable as machinery and tools under § 58.1-3507 Municipal Code N/A N/A
Isle of Wight County Full Certified pollution control equipment and facilities, as defined herein, are hereby declared to be a separate class of property and shall constitute a classification for local taxation separate from other such classification of real or personal property and such property shall be exempt from local taxation. Loudoun County Full 100% exemption for 10 years for projects 5MW or less. Greater than 5MW and less than 150MW follows Machinery & Tool tax. "If the certified solar energy equipment, facilities or devices are being taxed as real property, the amount of the exemption for each year shall be determined by applying the local real estate tax rate to the value of the certified solar energy equipment, facilities or devices and subtracting 100 percent of such amount from the total real estate property tax due on the real property to which such equipment, facilities or devices are attached, for a total period of ten years." Municipal Code loudoun.gov
